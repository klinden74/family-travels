The Itinerary

Banff

5 Nights in the Heart of the Canadian Rockies

Day 1 June 21, 2026

Arrival & Explore Banff Town

Travel: Fly into Calgary International Airport (YYC)
Drive: Calgary to Banff (~1.5 hours via Trans-Canada Highway)
Afternoon: Check in and explore the charming town of Banff
Evening: Stroll Banff Avenue, browse local shops, enjoy dinner in town
Day 2 June 22, 2026

Banff National Park

Full Day: Explore Canada's first national park
Must-See: Bow Falls, Tunnel Mountain, Sulphur Mountain Gondola
Lakes: Minnewanka Lake, Two Jack Lake, Vermilion Lakes
Wildlife: Keep an eye out for elk, bighorn sheep, and bears
Day 3 June 23, 2026

Lake Louise

Morning: Drive to Lake Louise (~45 minutes from Banff)
Activities: Walk the lakeshore trail, canoe on the turquoise waters
Hike: Plain of Six Glaciers or Lake Agnes Tea House trail
Dining: Meal at Fairmont Chateau Lake Louise
Nearby: Moraine Lake (arrive early to avoid crowds)
Day 4 June 24, 2026

Yoho National Park

Location: Cross into British Columbia (~30 min from Lake Louise)
Highlights: Emerald Lake, Natural Bridge, Takakkaw Falls
Meaning: "Yoho" is a Cree expression of awe and wonder
Hike: Iceline Trail or Emerald Lake Loop

Jasper

2 Nights in Canada's Largest Rocky Mountain Park

Day 5 June 25, 2026

Icefields Parkway

Scenic Drive: Transfer from Banff to Jasper via the legendary Icefields Parkway
Distance: 232 km (144 miles) of stunning mountain scenery
Stops: Bow Lake, Peyto Lake, Columbia Icefield, Athabasca Falls
Experience: Columbia Icefield Glacier Adventure or Skywalk
Evening: Arrive in Jasper, check in and explore the town
Day 6 June 26, 2026

Jasper National Park

Full Day: Explore the largest national park in the Canadian Rockies
Must-See: Maligne Lake, Spirit Island boat cruise, Maligne Canyon
Activities: Hiking, wildlife viewing, Jasper SkyTram
Dark Sky: Jasper is a Dark Sky Preserve - incredible stargazing
Day 7 June 27, 2026

Departure

Morning: Final morning in Jasper
Drive: Jasper to Edmonton International Airport (~4 hours)
Alternative: Drive back to Calgary via Icefields Parkway (~4.5 hours)
Fly Home

Planning Notes

Parks Canada Pass

A Discovery Pass covers entry to all national parks. Purchase in advance online or at the park gates.

Best Time to Visit

Late June offers long daylight hours, wildflowers blooming, and most trails open. Weather can be unpredictable - pack layers!

Lake Louise & Moraine Lake

These iconic lakes are extremely popular. Arrive before 7am or take the Parks Canada shuttle to avoid parking headaches.

Wildlife Safety

Bears are active in summer. Carry bear spray, make noise on trails, and never approach wildlife. Store food properly.
